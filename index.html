Recycling

According to the US Environmental Protection Agency, recycling is the process of taking what would normally be trash and reprocessing it to make new items or materials. Recycling is important because it has many positive effects on our environment.

Impact of Waste

In 2018, 146.1 Million tons of trash ended up in a landfill. Landfills are bad for the environment because they contribute to climate change through the production of methane, leach into groundwater, and negatively impact wildlife.

Impact of Recycling

One of the many benefits of recycling is that it saves energy. Using recycled materials saves the energy needed to collect raw materials, thus meaning reduced energy usage to make the same product. This also leads to decreased: pollution, use of resources, and burning of fossil fuels.

What to Recycle

Items that can be recycled include: Boxes, Cans, Cardboard Packaging, Paper, Plastic Containers, Paper Bags, and Books. Pro Tip: Anything with the recycling logo can be recycled!

New "old" Items

Do you ever wonder what is being made from recycled materials? Everyday things like water bottles, Kindle E-readers, portable chargers, office chairs, blankets, cat toys, vynils, rugs, watches, and many more items are being made from recycled materials.

Positive Effects

Recycling can have many positive effects not only on our environment, but also on our communities. A few examples of these benefits are, creating more jobs, reduces waste, saves energy, reduces pollution, and preserves natural resources.
